FAQ

Common questions

What's the difference between Basic Care and Advanced Care?

Basic Care is delivered by vet professionals (nurses or techs) via live video. They triage your pet's symptoms and tell you the next step. Advanced Care involves an AVS licensed vet doctor via live video who can formally diagnose and prescribe if needed — activated when Basic Care determines it's clinically necessary.

Is ConnectPaw legal in Singapore?

Yes. ConnectPaw operates in full compliance with the AVS (Animal & Veterinary Service) guidelines and the Vet Practice Bill 2026. All consults are logged, vet credentials are verified, and we maintain clear boundaries between teleadvice/teletriage and telemedicine that requires a Veterinary-Client-Patient Relationship (VCPR).

Are consultations done by video or text?

Both Basic Care and Advanced Care are conducted via live video consultation — not text chat. This allows our vets and vet professionals to actually see your pet, assess symptoms visually, and provide accurate guidance the way a real clinical setting would.

What happens if my pet needs emergency care?

If our vet professional determines your pet needs immediate physical emergency care, they'll direct you to the nearest emergency clinic right away. Your ConnectPaw consult fee is waived for that session — you pay nothing to us. Your pet's safety always comes first.

How do I get an assigned vet doctor?

You can link your existing clinic vet to your ConnectPaw account by uploading a recent prescription (we use OCR to auto-detect the vet's details). This establishes your VCPR and assigns that vet as your go-to for Advanced Care sessions — maintaining continuity of care.

Do you offer plans for businesses, clinics, or insurers?

Yes. We work with employers who want to include pet healthcare in their benefits, vet clinics looking to extend post-discharge support, and insurers interested in a white-label telehealth layer. Reach out via the form below or connect with us on LinkedIn.
